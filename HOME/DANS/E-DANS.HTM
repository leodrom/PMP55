Well, a little about myself

    I was born in the cold of winter; of course I do not remember it, but it was around January 7, 1976. If those digits make you think—that was Christmas. The years passed in Lviv, a city in Western Ukraine. I learned to read,   write and all the other smart things at secondary school #31. It is already 1997 and knowledge keeps filling my head; moreover I am finishing my studies at Lviv State University at the faculty of applied mathematics (that is what the mysterious sign PMP-55 means).
    Let's think logically. I study applied mathematics, I have the knowledge I gained at school, you are reading this page – so logically you are learning about me and I in turn provide that. It is not surprising one could call me a provider of myself. But why be surprised? Internet pages exist for this.
    In blood and sweat, often lacking sleep (not to mention physical, moral and spiritual food) I spent my free time at the computer (that's the thing you often connect the "TV" to that you are now staring at, I hope with a smile). Working with it I came to the conclusion – it's not only a typewriter but also a money-making device and, on the scale of the Internet, a source of interesting acquaintances.

The project I'm working on now

Let's start with the problem.
    Imagine a situation where you have a text file containing interesting information. But no matter how useful that information is, you can use it only if it is presented in a more convenient form (lists, trees, etc.). For this you have to write rather bulky code that correctly reads the data from the text file and places it into one structure or another. If you've faced such a problem you couldn't help noticing that reading the data is linked with another issue: who will check the correctness of the data. A person who has at least some idea about compiler principles will immediately think to sketch the syntax structure before starting work on the reader.

What we came up with.
    The problem above arose before you. You haven't written a PhD thesis about building compilers and you don't know how to solve it. We came to this idea. If you fully imagine the structure of the data you process you can present it as syntax diagrams.
    In our program we created an editor for visual construction of syntax diagrams. In our opinion it turned out very convenient. For example: the diagrams are "drawn" faster than you would do it on paper.
    One plus of this project is that it's written as a component for Borland Delphi. This allows it to be plugged into your software products.
    We hope this component will be used not only by us but also by those for whom solving this issue takes a lot of time.
