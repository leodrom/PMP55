HELLO!

Let's get acquainted. As you've probably noticed my name is Andrij Maxymovych, among friends simply Maksik. In the computer world I go by the nick Andy MAX. Now we are acquainted.
 

Now I'll tell how it all started (as you understand I'll be speaking about myself). It all began in the summer of 1975 (sounds cool, but as far as I guess it was quite simple). My parents had just graduated from "Polytech" and my father was serving his "sentence" at military training not far from the house where his future wife lived (that is my future mom) and (without telling me) he often visited her "as a guest" (I don't mind now). Less than nine months passed when on April 7, 1976 my brightly expressed individuality came into this world.

That's where it all began. My first favourite pastime was a pacifier (don't think that hobby has lasted until now). My cunning parents realised that to shut me up they had to give me something to eat or trick me with a pacifier. Then, like any normal boy, I was drawn to four-wheeled machines. My mom (credit where it's due) spent a whole year faithfully pushing me in a stroller. At the age of two or three I was taught to read (that was cool), and then they decided not to ruin my childhood and... untaught me 8-( . Stop (that's to myself)! I could talk about my "happy" childhood for a very long time, so let's skip a few years.

After kindergarten I went to school. Oh! What a school it was (it still exists). And its number was cool - 46 (46 = 7 * 6 + 4, where 7 is my birth date, 4 is the month and 6 is the last digit of the year). My favourite subjects at school were first mathematics and later computer science - I could never understand what other pupils didn't get in these subjects. And my so to speak "least favourite" were almost all the rest - I couldn't grasp what my classmates saw in them. Let's not dwell on school, though besides studying there is much to remember. So having "suffered" through 10 years of school (they tolerated me no less), I finished it. Rather well, by the way (I won't say exactly how).

Then my "thorny" path carried me to the Ivan Franko University (or in short "IF castle"). I became a student (the so-called "prisoner of IF castle") of the Faculty of Applied Mathematics and Informatics (feel the connection with school? :-). While I had no problems with computer science, mathematics simply stunned me (it was something distant and unclear).
